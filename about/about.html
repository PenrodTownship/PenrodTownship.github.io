I create experiences  that help people understand the world around them.

Sports journalism was my first obsession. A childhood fascination with daily newspapers and sports magazines led me to chase a career in storytelling. In high school, I dove deep into sports writing. In college, I pursued it aggressively, taking on every reporting assignment I could get from South Carolina’s largest papers.

By graduation, the market for sports journalists had become flooded. Yet there was a deep need for journalists who could design pages. After nearly a year of searching for work, a college advisor pointed me to a small paper in search of a news designer. I was hired, and the next several years unfolded quickly.

In three months, I advanced to a larger in-state paper. In another nine months, South Carolina’s largest daily, The State, reached out in need of a sports designer. It was a perfect match. I knew its sports section inside-out, and had held a number of part-time jobs in its newsroom while in college.

After some time at The State, I had a clear-eyed realization. While writing about sports was what drew me to journalism, I began to see other opportunities to reach a broader audience. Writing was just one way to tell a story. Design offered a large toolset that could be used to make storytelling more resonant. As a reporter, I could write one or two stories a day. As a designer, I could elevate several. Or, even better, I could use design to improve the entire reading experience.

Design became my new obsession. Over the next decade, it would carry me around the country to tell stories alongside some of journalism’s best design minds at The San Diego Union-Tribune, The New York Times, the Los Angeles Times and at Joe Zeff Design, a creative studio in New Jersey. The sum of those roles carried me to Minneapolis, where I now lead teams of story and product designers for the Star Tribune.

Along the way, I learned how to tell all kinds of individual stories. I adopted design-thinking practices to deeply engage audiences on screens and in print. I became an adherent of using deep research, iterative prototyping and user-testing to solve problems. I started to code to improve reader experiences. And I helped create shared systems and processes to scale design operations.

I believe in the power of analytical thinking to guide decision-making. But, more than that, I know — and feel deep down in my bones — that good design fuels good businesses.

Now, how can I help you tell your story?
